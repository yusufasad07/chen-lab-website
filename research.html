Research

Innovating biomaterials for cardiovascular regeneration

We integrate responsive materials, advanced fabrication, and mechanobiology to engineer therapies that heal the heart and vasculature. Our projects span material discovery to translational validation.

Resorbable Metallo-Elastomer Vascular Grafts

Metals • Elastomers • Hemodynamics

Designing hybrid metallo-elastomer scaffolds that match arterial compliance, reduce thrombosis, and resorb as native tissue remodels. We leverage computational flow modeling and in vivo implantation models.

  • Bioresorbable alloys
  • Computational hemodynamics
  • Animal studies

Piezoelectric Modulation of Endothelialization

Mechanobiology • Surface Science

Embedding piezoelectric microtextures into stent coatings to convert vascular motion into bioelectrical cues that orchestrate endothelial alignment, nitric oxide signaling, and anti-inflammatory responses.

  • Piezoelectric polymers
  • Electrochemical sensing
  • Endothelial biology

3D-Printed Cardiac Patches with Microchannels

3D Printing • Regenerative Medicine

Fabricating patient-specific cardiac patches with perfusable microchannels that support oxygen diffusion, cell viability, and electromechanical coupling post-myocardial infarction.

  • Bioinks
  • Microfluidics
  • Cardiac regeneration

Bioadhesives for Minimally Invasive Repair

Bioadhesives • Soft Robotics

Creating shear-thinning, photoreversible bioadhesives that seal vascular injuries via catheter delivery while supporting tissue integration and hemostasis.

  • Injectable polymers
  • Hemostasis
  • Catheter devices

Microphysiological Models of Vascular Healing

Organ-on-Chip • Systems Biology

Building perfused microvascular chips that emulate injury-repair cycles, enabling high-throughput testing of biomaterial-cell interactions and drug responses.

  • Organ-on-chip
  • Immune interactions
  • Predictive modeling

Data-Guided Biomaterial Design

Machine Learning • Design of Experiments

Leveraging machine learning to rapidly explore biomaterial design spaces, predict performance metrics, and inform iterative experimentation.

  • Predictive analytics
  • High-throughput screening
  • Design optimization
